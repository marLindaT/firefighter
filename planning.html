Fire Fighter Techniques

A repository of techniques and theory for aspiring firefighters

Welcome to firefighter techniques. This site is dedicated to all those who are pursuing a career as either a professional or volunteer firefighter. This site is the result of my pursuit of techniques and theory that firefighters employ in their daily activities. The chemistry and physics of fire and application to controlling and extinguishing fires is investigated.

Check out the curriculum link in the top navigation bar. The content is broken into three basic groups: Science, Techniques, and Planning.

Science is a section dedicated to laying the ground work on understanding what fire is. What are those orange flames we so often find ourselves staring into. What causes things to burn. What are the different types of flames, what is an oxidizing agent, what is the fire triangle, and how can we stop fire. These are some of the big questions that are answered in the Science section

Techniques focuses on explaining common methods that firefighters use to cobat and extinguish fires. Why do firefighters cut holes in the roof of a burning building. Why is venitlation important, what is a flashover and how can it be prevented or anticipated.

Planning is a general section that aims to give a broad understanding of building structure to those less familiar with it. Explored are the basics elements of structural design, building material, and services that are common in different building types.
